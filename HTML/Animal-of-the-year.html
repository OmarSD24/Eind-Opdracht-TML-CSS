Jaguars

Animal of The Year Winner

Fast Facts about Jaguars

Unique Feature

Strongest bite force of any big cat, able to crush turtle shells and skulls.

Habitat

Rainforests, swamps and grasslands in Central and South America.

Diet

Carnivorous—hunts deer, capybaras, fish, and even caimans.

Swimming Ability

They are excellent swimmers often hunt in water.

Summary

Jaguars (*Panthera onca*) are powerful big cats native to Central and South America, particularly thriving in rainforests, swamps, and areas near water. Known for their exceptional strength, they have the strongest bite of any big cat, allowing them to crush hard prey like turtle shells and even hunt caimans. Jaguars are skilled swimmers, with a diet that includes deer, fish, and capybaras. Despite their adaptability, they are currently Near Threatened due to habitat loss and poaching, although they hold cultural significance as symbols of strength in Indigenous cultures.
